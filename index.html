Problem Description

Open-pit mining operations face a critical challenge in truck-shovel allocation. The core problem is a conflict of interest: the Dispatch Center aims to maximize production and grade adherence, while the Haulage Fleet (often outsourced) aims to minimize fuel consumption and operational costs.

Traditional heuristic methods often fail to find a global optimum, leading to excessive queue times at shovels and suboptimal fuel usage. This thesis proposes a quantitative framework to resolve this bi-level decision-making process using Game Theory.

Economic Validation: NPV Proxy Framework

Haulage is a cost center, but its optimization directly impacts mine profitability. We utilize a standardized proxy NPV formula to quantify the economic impact of optimized dispatch decisions in real-time.

NPV = Σ [ (Ore Value per Ton − Haulage Cost per Ton) × Annual Production ] × Discount Factor
  • Ore Value per Ton: Derived from geological block models (controlled via slider).
  • Haulage Cost per Ton: Dynamic output of the Stackelberg equilibrium model.
  • Annual Production: Aggregate throughput under the optimized dispatch strategy.
  • Discount Factor: Accounts for WACC and time value of money over the project lifecycle (e.g., 5 years).
